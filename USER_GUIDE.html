👋Welcome

University of Cape Coast Financial Management System is the all-in-one financial management system for University of Cape Coast, University of Cape Coast. This guide walks you through every part of it — from logging in to closing the month — in plain language, with diagrams you can actually follow.

🔐
Built for controlDouble-entry, approvals, audit trail and a locked trial balance on every posting.
🔗

📤
Report-readyEvery report exports to Excel & PDF; statements email themselves.

🚀Getting started

Signing in

  1. Open the app in your browser. You will see the sign-in screen.
  2. Enter your issued username and password and click Sign In.
  3. First-time accounts use a temporary password — SBS-FMS will prompt you to change it under Settings → Change Password.
🔒
If you see an orange banner saying you are signed in with a default password, change it immediately. Default passwords must be rotated before go-live.

Your period

SBS-FMS works in monthly accounting periods (e.g. 2026-06). You post into an open period; once a month is reconciled it is closed/locked so nothing changes underneath you.

Roles

What you can see and do depends on your role (Admin, Finance, Viewer, etc.). Some actions — posting depreciation, year-end close, user management — are Admin-only.

🧭The layout & navigation

Three zones: the sidebar (left, all modules), the top bar (page title + action buttons + Export), and the workspace (centre).

The sidebar

  • Modules are grouped into collapsible sections — click a group header to expand or collapse it.
  • The new finance modules live together under the 💼 Finance Suite group, starting with the 🏠 Overview command center.
  • Click any item to open it. The active page is highlighted.
🌗
Prefer dark mode? Toggle it from the top bar / settings — every screen, including Mo and toasts, follows along.

Notifications

Confirmations and errors appear as toasts. When a form is open, important messages float to the top-centre in bold colour so you never miss them — click a toast to dismiss it.

Exporting anything

Most report screens show a ⬇ Export ▾ button (Excel / PDF / CSV). See Reports, export & import.

💸How the money flows

Everything you do eventually becomes a balanced journal in the General Ledger. Understand this one picture and the whole system makes sense.

Every action posts a balanced journal that rolls up into the financial statements.
⚖️
Golden rule: for every entry, total Debits = total Credits. The system refuses to post anything that does not balance, so your Trial Balance is always in agreement.

What "Dr / Cr" means in practice

When you…Debit (Dr)Credit (Cr)
Raise a sales invoiceReceivablesIncome
Receive customer paymentBankReceivables
Post a supplier billExpensePayables
Pay a supplierPayablesBank
Depreciate an assetDepreciation expenseAccumulated depreciation

🗂️Chart of accounts

Accounts are numbered by type. The first digit tells you what kind of account it is.

The account-code structure used throughout the system.

Account names and codes appear in dropdowns wherever you choose where money goes. When importing, you can reference an account by its code or its name.

📁Projects & grants

Set up every project, grant or fund you manage — with donor, budget and (where used) division. Projects are the backbone that budgets, commitments and spending hang off.

How to

  1. Open Projects and click + New.
  2. Enter code, title, donor/funder, budget and dates.
  3. Save. The project now appears in dropdowns across the app.
💡
Use Fund Receipts to record money received against a grant, and Donor Statements to report back to funders.

📊Budgets & vote on account

Capture annual and quarterly budgets per project/department, then track them live against commitments and actual spend.

How to

  1. Open Annual Budgets (or Quarterly) and enter/upload your figures.
  2. Spending and commitments draw down the available balance automatically.
  3. Use Virements / Transfers to reallocate budget between lines.
💡
Vote on Account lets you authorise interim spending before a full budget is approved.

📌Commitments

A commitment ring-fences budget for an expected cost (a contract, an order) before the money actually leaves. This is encumbrance accounting — it stops you over-spending.

How to

  1. Open Commitments → + New, pick the vendor, project and amount.
  2. The committed amount reduces the project’s available budget.
  3. When you pay against it, the commitment is drawn down; settle it fully and it closes.
⚖️
Commitments are memo/encumbrance entries — they protect budget. The actual Dr/Cr hits the ledger when you record the payment.

💳Payments & actuals

Record real expenditure — payment vouchers and actuals — with the correct expense account, project and any taxes withheld (WHT, WHVAT, UCF).

How to

  1. Open Payments / Actuals → + New.
  2. Choose the expense account, project, vendor and amount; the system computes withholding taxes.
  3. Submit for approval, then post. Posting writes the journal to the GL.
⚖️
A payment posts Dr Expense and the tax liabilities, Cr Bank — net of WHT/WHVAT/UCF which sit as liabilities until remitted.

📝Journal vouchers

For anything that is not a standard transaction — corrections, accruals, reclassifications — post a manual journal voucher.

How to

  1. Open Journal Vouchers → + New.
  2. Add lines: pick accounts and enter debits and credits.
  3. It must balance (Dr = Cr) before you can post.
💡
Repeating the same JV every month? Make it a recurring journal instead and let the system post it for you.

🛒Procurement (requisition → PO → GRN)

The buying journey is fully tracked, from request to receipt.

Raise a requisition, convert to a PO, and record a GRN when goods arrive.

How to

  1. Raise a Purchase Requisition for what a department needs.
  2. Convert/approve it into a Purchase Order with line items, quantities and prices.
  3. When the goods or services arrive, record a Goods Received Note (GRN) against the PO.
🔁
Low on stock? The Stores module can raise a draft PO for you automatically — see Reorder.

🔗3-way match & auto-bill

The Procurement Match screen is where buying meets paying. It compares what you Ordered (PO) with what you Received (GRN) and what you were Billed (AP) — and lets you create the supplier bill in one click.

The complete procure-to-pay chain, each step feeding the next.

How to

  1. Open Finance Suite → Procurement Match.
  2. For a received-but-unbilled PO, click Create Bill — choose the default expense account and whether to post immediately.
  3. The bill appears in Accounts Payable, and the row turns Matched when Ordered = Billed and a GRN exists.
🟢
Status badges: Matched · Received, not billed · Variance · Billed, no GRN · Ordered. A variance means the bill and the order disagree — investigate before paying.

🧾Accounts Receivable (money owed to you)

Bill customers, recognise income, track debtors and apply receipts.

The receivables lifecycle, from invoice to cash.

Tabs

  • Invoices — create, post, receive payment, raise a credit note, print.
  • Customers — the debtor list with outstanding balances and statements.
  • Debtor Aging — what is owed by age bucket (current → 90+ days).
  • Recurring — standing invoices that raise themselves (see Recurring).

How to raise & settle an invoice

  1. In Receivables → + New Invoice, pick the customer, add one or more lines (income account + amount), set dates, and tick post immediately.
  2. Send the customer their Statement (Excel/PDF or email) any time.
  3. When they pay, click Receive on the invoice and record the bank account — the balance clears.
⚖️
Invoice: Dr Receivables / Cr Income (+ Cr Output VAT if any). Receipt: Dr Bank / Cr Receivables. Credit note reverses the income.

💳Accounts Payable (money you owe)

Record supplier bills, track creditors, and pay them — the mirror image of Receivables.

The payables lifecycle, from bill to settlement.

Tabs

  • Bills — create (multi-line), post, pay, raise a debit note, print.
  • Vendors — creditor list with outstanding balances and statements.
  • Creditor Aging — what you owe by age bucket.
  • Recurring — standing bills (rent, utilities, subscriptions).

How to record & pay a bill

  1. In Payables → + New Bill, pick the vendor, add expense lines and the supplier invoice number; post it.
  2. Click Pay to settle (full or partial) from a bank account, or use Batch Pay for many at once.
  3. A Debit Note reduces what you owe (returns, corrections).
⚖️
Bill: Dr Expense / Cr Trade Payables. Payment: Dr Payables / Cr Bank.

⚡Batch settlement runs

Settle many documents in one posting — perfect for cheque runs and bulk lodgements.

How to

  1. In Payables click 💸 Batch Pay (or Receivables → 💰 Batch Receipt).
  2. Tick the bills/invoices to settle, adjust amounts if needed, choose the bank and date — watch the running total.
  3. Post once: every document is settled and a single voucher is created.
⚖️
Batch pay posts one PV (Dr Payables per bill / Cr Bank total); batch receipt posts one RV (Dr Bank total / Cr Receivables per invoice). Over-payments are blocked.

🔁Recurring AR / AP / journals

Anything that repeats — rent, subscriptions, retainers, depreciation, accruals — can be a template that posts itself on a schedule.

TypeWhereCreates
Recurring invoicesReceivables → RecurringSales invoices
Recurring billsPayables → RecurringSupplier bills
Recurring journalsFinance Suite → Recurring JournalsBalanced JVs

How to

  1. Create a template: set the frequency (Weekly → Annually), first due date, optional end date, amount/lines, and whether to auto-post.
  2. Click Generate Due — the system creates one document per period that has fallen due (it catches up if you missed some).
  3. Pause a template any time; it is skipped until you re-activate it.
📅
If a generated document falls in a closed period, it is left as a draft and the schedule waits — re-run after you open the period.

📦Stores & inventory (with reorder)

Track stock items, receive and issue stock at weighted-average cost, and reorder before you run out.

How to

  1. Create items with a unit, reorder level and the inventory & expense accounts.
  2. Receive stock (Dr Inventory / Cr Bank) and Issue it to consumption (Dr Expense / Cr Inventory).
  3. Click 🔄 Reorder to see everything at/below its reorder level, then raise a draft PO for the shortfalls in one click.
⚖️
Weighted-average costing. Issues are valued at the running average. Over-issues (more than on hand) are blocked.
💡
The reorder PO flows straight into Procurement Match → GRN → bill. The loop is closed.

🏛️Fixed assets

Manage the asset register across its whole life.

The fixed-asset lifecycle.
  • Register assets with cost, useful life, category and custodian.
  • Run Depreciation (Admin) posts the month’s depreciation across all assets at once — Dr Depreciation expense / Cr Accumulated depreciation — and is guarded against running twice for the same month.
  • Dispose an asset (sell or write-off): the system books the proceeds, removes cost and accumulated depreciation, and posts the gain or loss automatically.
⚖️
Disposal: Dr Accumulated depreciation + Dr Bank (proceeds) + Dr Loss (if any) = Cr Asset cost + Cr Gain (if any).

👥Payroll

Run staff payroll with PAYE, SSNIT (Tier 1 & 2) and other deductions, then post it to the ledger and print compliant payslips.

How to

  1. Maintain employees and their pay elements.
  2. Run payroll for the period, review, and approve.
  3. Approving posts the payroll journal; payslips and statutory schedules are then available.
⚖️
Payroll posts gross pay to expense, with net pay and each statutory deduction (PAYE, SSNIT, etc.) sitting as liabilities until paid/remitted.

🔮Cash forecast

A forward look at liquidity: opening cash, expected inflows (receivables due) and outflows (payables & commitments due), and the projected running balance by time bucket.

How to

  1. Open Finance Suite → Cash Forecast.
  2. Read the projected balance across the buckets (overdue → 90+ days).
  3. Spot a dip early and act — chase a debtor or delay a payment.

📊Working capital

A live snapshot of short-term financial health: cash, receivables, payables and inventory, with Net Working Capital and the current & quick ratios.

How to

  1. Open Finance Suite → Working Capital.
  2. Net working capital = current assets − current liabilities; a current ratio above 1.0× means you can cover short-term obligations.
💡
The AR-vs-AP aging snapshot here shows, at a glance, who owes you and who you owe by age.

📄Month-end flash pack

A one-page executive summary for the board: financial position, income vs expenditure (period & year-to-date), trial-balance health, working capital, aging and top expenditure.

How to

  1. Open Finance Suite → Month-End Flash, pick the period and Load.
  2. Review on screen, then 🖨 Print Flash Pack for a clean one-pager (or PDF).

📈Trends

See income vs expenditure and surplus/deficit over recent months as a quick chart plus a table — useful for spotting direction at a glance.

How to

  1. Open Finance Suite → Trends. Green bars are income, red are expenditure.

🧾Statutory tax pack

All your statutory deductions for a period in one place — WHT, VAT-withholding, PAYE, SSNIT and the UCC Common Fund — showing what was accrued, what was remitted and what is still outstanding.

How to

  1. Open Finance Suite → Tax Pack, pick the period and Load.
  2. Use the outstanding figures to prepare your GRA/SSNIT remittances; export each schedule to Excel/PDF.
💡
Pair this with Payments / Actuals (which captures WHT at source) and Payroll (PAYE/SSNIT) for complete returns.

📨Debtor reminders & emailing statements

Chase overdue customers without the manual grind. Dunning lists everyone overdue and emails each a statement reminder.

How to

  1. Open Finance Suite → Debtor Reminders to see who is overdue and by how long.
  2. Click Send reminders to all (or remind one customer) — each gets their statement by email.
  3. You can also email any statement directly from the AR/AP statement screen via the ✉️ Email button.
💡
Emailing needs SMTP configured by your admin. Until then, reminders are safely queued (nothing is sent).

🏦Bank reconciliation

Tie your cashbook to the bank. Import a bank statement, auto-match it to your records, and clear the differences so the reconciled balance agrees.

How to

  1. Open Bank Reconciliation and pick the account and period.
  2. Import the bank statement (CSV) — the system auto-matches lines by reference and amount.
  3. Resolve unmatched items (bank charges, timing) so the difference is zero, then save.
⚖️
Bank charges and other adjustments are posted to the cashbook side so the reconciliation lands exactly at zero.

📤Reports, export & import

Get data out — and in — effortlessly.

Export anything

Wherever you see ⬇ Export ▾, choose Excel (.xlsx), PDF or CSV. Statements have their own Excel/PDF buttons. The files are real Office documents, ready to share.

Bulk import

Loading a list of debtor invoices or creditor bills? Use ⬆ Import in Receivables/Payables. Upload Excel (.xlsx), Word (.docx, first table) or CSV — download the built-in template so your columns line up. Customers/vendors are matched (or created), accounts resolved by code or name, and rows become drafts (or auto-post).

🧩
Required columns are tiny: for debtors just customer and amount; for creditors vendor and amount. Everything else is optional.

🤖Mo — your AI finance assistant

Meet Mo, the friendly assistant tucked in the bottom-right corner. Mo knows double-entry accounting, Ghana tax rules and every module in SBS-FMS.

Opening Mo

Click the round avatar button (bottom-right). Mo’s panel slides up with a greeting and quick-action chips.

What Mo does

  • Briefing mode — even without AI configured, Mo briefs you on live numbers: cash position, pending approvals, receivables outstanding, overdue withholdings, low stock and more.
  • Ask anything — “How do I record a withholding payment?”, “What accounts do I debit for a disposal?”, “Show me overdue taxes.” Type it in and send.
  • Quick chips — one-tap shortcuts like Cash position, Pending approvals, Overdue taxes.
🧠
Full conversational answers switch on when an administrator sets ANTHROPIC_API_KEY on the server. The live briefing works regardless.
💬
Stuck on a screen? Open Mo and describe what you want to do — it will point you to the right module and the correct accounts.

✅Month-end close playbook

A clean, repeatable sequence to close a period with confidence.

The recommended month-end close order.
  1. Post all transactions for the month (AP bills, AR invoices, payments, payroll).
  2. Generate recurring invoices, bills and journals that fell due.
  3. Run depreciation for the month.
  4. Complete bank reconciliation for every account.
  5. Review the Tax Pack and prepare remittances; chase debtors via Dunning.
  6. Check Working Capital and print the Month-End Flash for management.
  7. When everything ties, close/lock the period so no further postings slip in.
🏁
The Finance Suite → Overview "Needs attention" bar is your pre-close checklist: clear the alerts and you are ready.

🛡️Security & go-live

A few one-time settings make the system production-ready. These are deliberately left to you and your administrator.

ActionWhy
Rotate all default passwordsStops anyone signing in with the shipped credentials.
Set SMTP (HOST/PORT/USER/PASSWORD/FROM)Enables sending statements & dunning emails.
Configure backups & enable dual-controlProtects data and enforces maker-checker on large entries.
🔐
Until passwords are rotated you will see the orange banner. The Go-Live Readiness screen shows a green/red checklist of exactly what is outstanding.

Always-on protections

  • Every change is written to the audit trail (who, what, when).
  • Postings to closed periods are blocked.
  • Unbalanced journals are rejected — the trial balance can never drift.
